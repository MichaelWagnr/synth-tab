Equipment

Synths and Samplers I love

Roland Juno 106

1983

A bandmate of mine purchased a Juno around 2016, that I borrowed for shows. I was really fortunate to have a roommate that was getting rid of a quite damaged Juno around the same time. He sold it to me for $250!

Over the course of several months I replaced just about every part worth replacing while crossing my fingers hoping that it would work.

When I finally plugged it in for the final test the cutoff filter was 100% closed, producing no sound whatsoever. I thought all my attempts were in vain until I noticed the slider position and toggled it open.

I can't say that I did a perfect job restoring it - some keys are unresponsive - but I can plug into it with a midi cable and play it that way.

Roland Sh-101

1982

I was introduced to electronic music at a young age watching flash animations on the internet circa 2000. A number of them had groups like Aphex Twin and Boards of Canada as the soundtrack.

Around 2014, I was promoted to a managerial position at a warehouse. The pay increase was great and I rewarded myself with a new synthesizer.

Most vintage polyphonic synths were twice as expensive or more as most vintage monophonic synthesizers. So I popped on eBay and nabbed an SH101.

It's my favourite synthesizer. It perfectly encapsulates that nostalgic sound of my youth listening to IDM on the early internet.

Akai MPC1000

2003

A few years into making electronic music, I went deep into samplers. I was possibly more interested in samplers then synthesizers, I certainly am now.

I used to own a couple rack-mounted Akai s3000xl samplers, which were quite unruly to gig with.

At a show in Edmonton I watched someone perform a techno P.A. on an Mpc1000. I thought MPCs were strictly used for Hip Hop production.

I was disapointed to find a huge gap between the functionality between the rack samplers and the Mpc1k. However with the JJOS 2XL firmware update, it transforms into an absolute powerhouse which I primarily use for sampled synth sounds!

Elektron Digitakt

2017

2019, I went to a show in the garmet district of Montreal. Watching DJ's perform on two decks, I got a weird idea to purchase a second sampler, and a 2 channel mixer with a cross-fader and perform on them as if they were record players.

I abandonned that idea pretty quickly. However, the grouping of the Mpc1000 and Digitakt proved very advantageous.

The Digitakt, although often used as a stand-alone sampler, really shines as a drum machine. The Mpc1000, although capable of being a drum machine, has a number of envelopes and polyphony that place it in a category outside of the Digitakt's use-case.

Both together, incredible.

Yamaha DX100

1985

FM synthesis. It's a chore to learn how to make sounds with it. I owned a few DX7s and a TX81z at various points.

I opted for the DX100 because of it's small form-factor. It also has this pretty disctinctive digital-aliasing that makes it sound very gritty.

There's a video on youtube of someone playing custom patches on the DX100 and a few of them sound a lot like Boards of Canada

I was able to get really close to the opening pad sound of Boards of Canada's Sunshine Recorder by slightly altering a Pan Flute preset - as I imagine they might have were they to own a DX100. This is quite possible, considering their affinity for flutes and the popularity/availability of the DX100 at the time of this songs recording.
